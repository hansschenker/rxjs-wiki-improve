Algebra is primarily concerned with creating, manipulating, and evaluating expressions. These expressions are built using variables, constants, and operators. Programmers often visualize expressions as trees

𝑥2 + 2𝑥 + 1

This expression is formed using variables like 𝑥, and constants like 1 or
2, bound together with operators like plus or times. As programmers,
we often think of expressions as trees.



To a mathematician, monads are about algebras

an algebra is defined by a set of n-ary operations (functions that take n arguments) 
and a set of equational identities that these operations must satisfy. 
These identities are universally quantified (e.g., an associativity equation must hold for all combinations of three elements). 
This definition can be extended from sets to other categories by replacing operations (functions) with morphisms.
