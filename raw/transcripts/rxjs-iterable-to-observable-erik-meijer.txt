what are the four essential effects in programming
The video explains four essential effects in programming, connected through dualization and category theory (8:46-9:38):

Computation Failure (Try of T): This effect signifies that a computation can fail (8:46-8:56).
Asynchronous Computation with Failure (Futures): Represents a computation that takes time and can also fail. Futures are shown to be connected to the "Try of T" concept through dualization (8:56-9:10).
Synchronous Collections (Iterables): This refers to collections of values where you "pull" or block until the next value is available (9:10-9:22).
Asynchronous Push-Based Sequences (Observables): This is the dual of iterables, representing sequences of values delivered asynchronously (9:22-9:28).
