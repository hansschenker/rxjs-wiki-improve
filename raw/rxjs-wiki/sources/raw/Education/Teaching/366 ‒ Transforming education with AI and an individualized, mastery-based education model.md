- The current education system is time-based, where students move to the next grade level at the end of each year, regardless of their mastery of the subject matter, and this has led to a lack of emphasis on mastery, with students often being passed to the next grade even if they have not fully grasped the material [(00:21:00)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1260s).
- In this system, grades and standardized test scores have become inflated, with 80% of kids at Harvard getting an A, and this has created a situation where students are moving on to more advanced subjects without having a solid foundation in the basics, leading to gaps in knowledge that compound over time [(00:21:19)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1279s).
- The concept of mastery is similar to how sports teams approach player development, where a player who is struggling with a basic skill, such as dribbling, is not moved on to more advanced skills, like dunking, until they have mastered the basics, and this approach can be applied to academics as well [(00:22:17)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1337s).
- In a time-based system with a teacher in front of a classroom, it is difficult for every student to achieve mastery, and this is where new solutions, such as personalized learning and artificial intelligence, can help, by allowing students to learn at their own pace and focus on filling gaps in their knowledge [(00:23:12)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1392s).
- The education system is hierarchical, with more advanced subjects building on the foundations of earlier ones, such as chemistry being built on algebra, and physics being built on calculus, and fractions being a key predictor of algebra scores, and to fix the system, it is necessary to understand the relationship between age grade and knowledge grade [(00:23:30)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1410s).

- The current system can be limiting, and a new approach that focuses on mastery and individualized learning can help students learn more efficiently and effectively, by allowing them to work at their own pace and focus on mastering the basics before moving on to more advanced subjects [(00:24:23)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=1463s).

## The potential to reduce inequality in academic success [(01:44:14)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2g2n_CkAolg&t=6254s)
